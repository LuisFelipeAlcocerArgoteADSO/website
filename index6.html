Critical Thinking and Media Literacy

Media and Information Literacy (MIL) plays a significant role in enhancing critical thinking skills.
Here are some ways it does so:
-Differentiating Facts and Opinions: MIL helps individuals distinguish between facts and opinions.
-Evaluating Data: It encourages individuals to always evaluate the data they come across.
-Fact-Checking: MIL promotes the habit of verifying the truthfulness of information through fact-checking.
-Using Credible Sources: It guides individuals to refer only to credible sources for information.
-Avoiding Single Source Dependency: MIL advises not to rely on a single source for information.

Research shows that media and information literacy improves critical thinking,
awareness of media bias, and the desire to consume quality news. It's also found
that there are connections between media literacy instruction and critical thinking skills.
Media literacy education is a tool for combating mis/disinformation in a time where there is so much uncertainty.
This education encourages readers to consider the use of these media literacy skills in educational and community
settings to help the public better understand how to manage various mediated environments3.
